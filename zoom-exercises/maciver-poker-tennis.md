# Zoom Exercise Ideas: Life is Neither Poker nor Tennis

**Reference:** [Life is Neither Poker nor Tennis - David MacIver](https://notebook.drmaciver.com/posts/2020-05-28-07:42.html)
**Topic:** Critique of game metaphors for life; need for metarationality
**Duration:** 10 minutes

## Core Themes
- Poker and tennis are poor models for life
- Different kinds of agency, structure, uncertainty
- Metarationality vs. simple rationality
- Life doesn't have clear rules, opponents, or endpoints
- Danger of over-generalizing from specific games

## Exercise 1: Game Metaphor Critique (35 minutes)

Examine how game metaphors shape thinking about life

### Activity
1. Identify Common Game Metaphors (10 min): Life as chess, war, race, etc.
2. Analyze Limitations (20 min): What does each metaphor hide?
3. Synthesis (5 min): Better alternatives?

---

## Exercise 2: Poker vs. Tennis vs. Life (30 minutes)

Compare structural features

### Activity
1. Chart Features (15 min): Rules, opponents, endpoints, luck, skill
2. Map Life Features (10 min): How does life differ?
3. Discussion (5 min): What's lost in the metaphor?

---

## Exercise 3: Metarationality Exploration (35 minutes)

Practice MacIver's metarationality

### Activity
1. Rational Approach (10 min): Solve problem rationally
2. Metarational Approach (15 min): Question the problem itself
3. Comparison (10 min): What shifted?

---

## Exercise 4: Agency Spectrum (30 minutes)

Explore different kinds of agency in poker, tennis, and life

### Activity
1. Agency Analysis (20 min): How is agency different in each?
2. Life Application (10 min): Where do you have what kind of agency?

---

## Exercise 5: Beyond Game Thinking (25 minutes)

Develop non-game frameworks for life

### Activity
1. Brainstorm Alternatives (15 min): What's better than game metaphors?
2. Test Application (10 min): Apply to real life situation
