# Zoom Exercise Ideas: Richard Feynman, Spinning Plates and Serious Play

**Reference:** [Richard Feynman, Spinning Plates and Serious Play - Ben Weinlick](https://thinkjarcollective.com/articles/richard-feynman-spinning-plates-and-serious-play/)
**Topic:** Feynman's creative process through serious play
**Duration:** 6 minutes

## Core Themes
- Domain knowledge + focus + letting go + serious play = creativity
- Play as recovery from burnout
- Following curiosity without justification
- Spinning plates story as archetype
- Permission to play leads to Nobel Prize

## Exercise 1: The Feynman Pattern (30 minutes)

Practice: Domain Knowledge → Focus → Letting Go → Serious Play → Breakthrough

### Activity
1. Present the Pattern (5 min)
2. Apply to Problem (20 min): Choose real challenge, work through pattern
3. Reflection (5 min): What emerged?

---

## Exercise 2: Permission to Play (25 minutes)

Explore needing/giving yourself permission for purposeless exploration

### Activity
1. Constraint Period (8 min): Work on something "productive"
2. Permission Period (12 min): Play with same topic, no outcomes required
3. Comparison (5 min): What changed with permission?

---

## Exercise 3: Curiosity Following (30 minutes)

Practice following curiosity wherever it leads, like Feynman with spinning plates

### Activity
1. Start with anything that interests you
2. Follow tangents for 15 minutes without justification
3. Map where curiosity led
4. Discuss: What connections emerged?

---

## Exercise 4: Burnout and Play (25 minutes)

Examine play as response to creative burnout

### Activity
1. Burnout Assessment (10 min)
2. Play Prescription (10 min): What playful exploration would restore you?
3. Barriers Discussion (5 min): What prevents play recovery?

---

## Exercise 5: Serious vs. Justified Play (20 minutes)

Feynman stopped justifying his play. Can you?

### Activity
1. List activities you feel you must justify
2. Practice unjustified play
3. Discuss difficulty of purposeless engagement
