# Zoom Exercise Ideas: What's the Point

**Reference:** [What's the Point - Anon](https://clinicalphilosophy.blogspot.com/2016/12/whats-the-point.html)
**Topic:** Depression, alienation, and the limits of point-seeking
**Duration:** 5 minutes

## Core Themes
- Depression rests on alienation from life
- "What's the point" traps us in depressive conversation
- Many activities neither pointful nor pointless
- Alternative questions disrupt depressive rumination
- Play as non-point-seeking engagement

## Exercise 1: The Point-Seeking Trap (30 minutes)

Experience how "what's the point" creates paralysis

### Activity
1. Point-Seeking Mode (10 min): Do things only if you can articulate point
2. Non-Point Mode (10 min): Do things without needing point
3. Comparison (10 min): Which was more depressive?

---

## Exercise 2: Neither Pointful Nor Pointless (25 minutes)

Explore third category beyond point/pointless dichotomy

### Activity
1. List Activities (10 min): Categorize as pointful, pointless, or neither
2. Analysis (10 min): What's the "neither" category?
3. Insights (5 min): Is play in this category?

---

## Exercise 3: Alternative Questions (35 minutes)

Practice questions that disrupt "what's the point"

### Activity
1. Point-Question Depression (10 min): Keep asking "what's the point"
2. Alternative Questions (15 min): Ask "what's interesting?" "what's alive?" "what's next?"
3. Comparison (10 min): How did questions shape experience?

---

## Exercise 4: Play as Non-Point Activity (30 minutes)

Engage in play without needing point

### Activity
1. Justify Play (10 min): Try to explain point of playing
2. Just Play (10 min): Play without justification
3. Reflection (10 min): Which was more playful?

---

## Exercise 5: Alienation and Engagement (25 minutes)

Explore how play reconnects when alienated

### Activity
1. Notice Alienation (8 min): Where do you feel disconnected?
2. Playful Engagement (12 min): Can play reconnect you?
3. Insights (5 min)
