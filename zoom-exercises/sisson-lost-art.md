# Zoom Exercise Ideas: The Lost Art of Play

**Reference:** [The Lost Art of Play - Mark Sisson](https://web.archive.org/web/20231220072239/http://www.marksdailyapple.com/the-lost-art-of-play-reclaiming-a-primal-tradition/)
**Topic:** Why adults should play; physical play as primal need
**Duration:** 10 minutes

## Core Themes
- Physical play as evolutionary need
- Adults have lost play capacity
- Benefits of adult physical play
- Recovery of primal play patterns
- Play vs. exercise distinction

## Exercise 1: Physical Play Audit (30 minutes)

Assess your current physical play

### Activity
1. Inventory (10 min): When do you engage in physical play vs. exercise?
2. Pattern Analysis (15 min): What changed from childhood?
3. Recovery Plan (5 min): How to restore physical play?

---

## Exercise 2: Play vs. Exercise (30 minutes)

Experience the difference

### Activity
1. Exercise Mode (10 min): Goal-oriented physical activity
2. Play Mode (10 min): Playful physical engagement
3. Comparison (10 min): How did each feel in body and mind?

---

## Exercise 3: Primal Play Patterns on Zoom (25 minutes)

Adapt physical play to Zoom context

### Activity
1. Identify Primal Patterns (8 min): Running, jumping, climbing, throwing
2. Zoom Adaptations (12 min): How to play physically on video?
3. Test (5 min): Try them

---

## Exercise 4: Adult Play Barriers (35 minutes)

Identify what prevents adult physical play

### Activity
1. Barrier Identification (15 min): Social, physical, psychological
2. Mitigation Strategies (15 min): How to overcome?
3. Commitment (5 min)

---

## Exercise 5: Play Integration (20 minutes)

Design regular physical play practice

### Activity
1. Current State (5 min)
2. Play Design (12 min): Weekly physical play practices
3. Implementation (3 min)
