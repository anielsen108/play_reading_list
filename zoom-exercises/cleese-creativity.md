# Zoom Exercise Ideas: Lecture On Creativity

**Reference:** [Lecture On Creativity - John Cleese](http://genius.com/John-cleese-lecture-on-creativity-annotated#note-2556672)
**Topic:** Open mode vs. closed mode thinking for creativity
**Duration:** 15 minutes

## Core Themes
- Open mode: Playful, exploratory, creative, relaxed
- Closed mode: Focused, decisive, productive, stressed
- Both necessary but used at wrong times
- Creativity requires open mode
- Specific conditions for accessing open mode

## Exercise 1: Open vs. Closed Mode Experience (30 minutes)

Directly experience both modes

### Activity
1. Closed Mode (10 min): Solve problems efficiently, time pressure
2. Open Mode (10 min): Explore playfully, no pressure
3. Comparison (10 min): How did each feel? What did each produce?

---

## Exercise 2: Cleese's Five Conditions (40 minutes)

Practice creating conditions for open mode (Space, Time, Time, Confidence, Humor)

### Activity
1. Condition Explanation (10 min)
2. Create Conditions on Zoom (20 min)
3. Test Creativity (10 min): Did open mode emerge?

---

## Exercise 3: Mode-Switching Practice (25 minutes)

Learn to deliberately switch between modes

### Activity
1. Practice entering open mode (8 min)
2. Practice entering closed mode (7 min)
3. Rapid switching (10 min): Back and forth

---

## Exercise 4: Organizational Mode Audit (30 minutes)

Examine which mode dominates your work/life

### Activity
1. Time audit (10 min): % in open vs. closed
2. Optimization (15 min): Where are you using wrong mode?
3. Redesign (5 min): How to access open mode more?

---

## Exercise 5: Humor and Creativity (25 minutes)

Explore Cleese's emphasis on humor in creativity

### Activity
1. Serious Creative Attempt (8 min)
2. Humorous Creative Attempt (8 min)
3. Comparison (9 min): Did humor unlock more creativity?
