# Zoom Exercise Ideas: Agency Made Me Do It - Play

**Reference:** [Agency Made Me Do It: Play - Mike Travers](http://www.hyperphor.com/ammdi/pages/play.html)
**Topic:** Play and agency; play as non-goal-oriented
**Duration:** 8 minutes

## Core Themes
- Play challenges standard rational agency model
- Play is not goal-oriented (or has different goals)
- Play-goals different from rationality-goals
- Play as distinct mode of agency
- Relevance to any theory of agency

## Exercise 1: Rational vs. Play Agency (30 minutes)

Experience different modes of agency

### Activity
1. Rational Agency (10 min): Act toward clear goals
2. Play Agency (10 min): Act without pre-determined goals
3. Comparison (10 min): How did agency feel different?

---

## Exercise 2: Goal Types (35 minutes)

Explore different kinds of goals in play vs. work

### Activity
1. Identify Work Goals (10 min): External, predetermined
2. Identify Play Goals (10 min): Emergent, intrinsic
3. Analysis (15 min): Are these the same kind of thing?

---

## Exercise 3: Agency Models (30 minutes)

Compare agency frameworks

### Activity
1. Standard Model (10 min): Goals → Actions → Outcomes
2. Play Model (10 min): Actions → Emergent patterns → Retroactive meaning
3. Discussion (10 min): Which better describes your life?

---

## Exercise 4: Non-Goal-Oriented Action (25 minutes)

Practice acting without predetermined goals

### Activity
1. Purposive Action (8 min): Clear goal, efficient means
2. Playful Action (8 min): No goal, follow interest
3. Reflection (9 min): What kind of agency is play?

---

## Exercise 5: Agency Theory Implications (20 minutes)

Consider what play means for understanding agency

### Activity
1. Current Agency Model (8 min)
2. Play-Inclusive Model (12 min): How would it differ?
