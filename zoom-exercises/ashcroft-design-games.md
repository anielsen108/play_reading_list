# Zoom Exercise Ideas: Design Games So You Enjoy Playing Them

**Reference:** [Design games so you enjoy playing them - Michael Ashcroft](https://www.michaelashcroft.org/notebook/design-games-so-you-enjoy-playing-them)
**Topic:** Play as state of mind; enjoying process vs. outcome
**Duration:** 5 minutes

## Core Themes
- Play as approach, not activity type
- Design your life games to be enjoyable
- Process focus vs. outcome focus
- You have choice in how you frame activities
- Life as infinite game you can redesign

## Exercise 1: Game Redesign (35 minutes)

Redesign something you hate into something playful

### Activity
1. Identify Unpleasant Activity (5 min)
2. Analyze Current "Rules" (10 min): Why is it unpleasant?
3. Redesign (15 min): Change rules to make it playful
4. Share (5 min)

---

## Exercise 2: Process vs. Outcome Focus (30 minutes)

Practice shifting to process enjoyment

### Activity
1. Outcome-Focused Round (10 min): Care only about results
2. Process-Focused Round (10 min): Enjoy the doing
3. Comparison (10 min): Which was more playful?

---

## Exercise 3: Your Current Life Games (30 minutes)

Audit the games you're playing

### Activity
1. Identify Games (10 min): Work, relationships, health, etc.
2. Enjoyment Rating (10 min): Do you enjoy playing them?
3. Redesign Options (10 min): How could you change the rules?

---

## Exercise 4: Infinite vs. Finite Games (25 minutes)

Apply James Carse's framework

### Activity
1. Finite Games (8 min): Games to win/end
2. Infinite Games (8 min): Games to keep playing
3. Life Application (9 min): Which are you playing?

---

## Exercise 5: The Meta-Game (20 minutes)

Play with the game of designing games

### Activity
1. Design a Game Design Practice (15 min)
2. Share Designs (5 min)
