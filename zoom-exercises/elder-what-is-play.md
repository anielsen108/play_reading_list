# Zoom Exercise Ideas: What is Play? (Matt Elder)

**Reference:** [What is Play? - Matt Elder](https://twitter.com/fiddlemath/status/1207404931776315392)
**Topic:** Explorations of "What is play?"
**Duration:** 15 minutes (Twitter thread)

## Core Themes
- Multiple perspectives on defining play
- Play as emergent phenomenon
- Difficulty of pinning down essence of play
- Various frameworks and approaches
- Phenomenological exploration

## Exercise 1: Personal Play Definitions (30 minutes)

Develop your own definition through exploration

### Activity
1. Individual Definition (10 min): What is play for you?
2. Compare Definitions (15 min): How do they differ?
3. Synthesis (5 min): Common elements?

---

## Exercise 2: Multiple Frameworks (35 minutes)

Explore play through different lenses

### Activity
1. Assign Frameworks (5 min): Psychological, biological, cultural, phenomenological
2. Analyze Play (20 min): Same activity through each lens
3. Integration (10 min): What does each reveal?

---

## Exercise 3: Edge Cases (30 minutes)

Test definitions with ambiguous cases

### Activity
1. Present Edge Cases (10 min): Is this play?
2. Debate (15 min): Apply different definitions
3. Reflection (5 min): What do disagreements reveal?

---

## Exercise 4: Phenomenological Approach (25 minutes)

Describe play from inside the experience

### Activity
1. Play Engagement (10 min)
2. Immediate Description (10 min): What did it feel like?
3. Essence Identification (5 min): What made it play?

---

## Exercise 5: Synthesis Attempt (20 minutes)

Try to create comprehensive definition

### Activity
1. Elements Collection (10 min)
2. Integration (10 min): Combine into working definition
