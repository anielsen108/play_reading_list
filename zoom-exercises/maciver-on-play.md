# Zoom Exercise Ideas: On Play (David MacIver)

**Reference:** [On Play - David MacIver](https://twitter.com/DRMacIver/status/1340636936340430849)
**Topic:** Considerations on "what is play?"
**Duration:** 10 minutes (Twitter thread)

## Core Themes
- Play as exploration without commitment
- Safety and play relationship
- Play as low-stakes experimentation
- Social dimensions of play
- Play as distinct from both work and leisure

## Exercise 1: Safety and Play (30 minutes)

Explore how safety enables play

### Activity
1. Unsafe Context (10 min): Try to play under threat
2. Safe Context (10 min): Play in supportive environment
3. Comparison (10 min): How did safety affect play?

---

## Exercise 2: Exploration Without Commitment (25 minutes)

Practice MacIver's "exploration without commitment"

### Activity
1. High-Commitment Exploration (8 min): Decisions matter
2. No-Commitment Exploration (8 min): Nothing binding
3. Reflection (9 min): How did commitment level affect exploration?

---

## Exercise 3: Play vs. Work vs. Leisure (35 minutes)

Distinguish play from related concepts

### Activity
1. Create Venn Diagram (15 min): Play, work, leisure - overlaps?
2. Place Activities (15 min): Where does each belong?
3. Insights (5 min): How is play distinct?

---

## Exercise 4: Social Play (30 minutes)

Examine play's social dimensions

### Activity
1. Solo Play (10 min)
2. Social Play (10 min)
3. Comparison (10 min): How does social context change play?

---

## Exercise 5: Low-Stakes Practice (20 minutes)

Use play for low-stakes experimentation

### Activity
1. Identify High-Stakes Area (5 min)
2. Create Low-Stakes Play Version (10 min)
3. Test (5 min)
