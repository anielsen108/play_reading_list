# Zoom Exercise Ideas: Todd Hargrove on Play and Personal Autonomy

**Reference:** [Excerpt from Todd Hargrove on Play and Personal Autonomy](https://twitter.com/Meaningness/status/1247333001618313217)
**Topic:** How those who do not play lose personal autonomy
**Duration:** 5 minutes (Twitter excerpt)

## Core Themes
- Play as practice of self-direction
- Loss of play = loss of autonomy
- External direction becomes default
- Play teaches you to make your own choices
- Connection to mental health and agency

## Exercise 1: Autonomy Audit (30 minutes)

Examine relationship between play and autonomy in your life

### Activity
1. Map Activities (10 min): Plot on autonomy spectrum
2. Correlate with Play (15 min): Do playful activities have more autonomy?
3. Insights (5 min): What's the relationship?

---

## Exercise 2: Play and Decision-Making (25 minutes)

Explore how play practices decision-making

### Activity
1. Directed Activity (8 min): Follow exact instructions
2. Playful Autonomy (8 min): Make all your own choices
3. Comparison (9 min): How does choice feel?

---

## Exercise 3: Recovering Autonomy Through Play (35 minutes)

Practice using play to rebuild autonomy

### Activity
1. Identify Low-Autonomy Area (10 min)
2. Design Playful Approach (15 min): How to introduce choice/play?
3. Test and Reflect (10 min)

---

## Exercise 4: The Non-Play Trajectory (20 minutes)

Imagine development without play

### Activity
1. Thought Experiment (10 min): Life with no playful practice
2. Discussion (10 min): How would autonomy develop?

---

## Exercise 5: Play as Autonomy Practice (25 minutes)

Design ongoing practices

### Activity
1. Current Autonomy Level (8 min)
2. Play Interventions (12 min): What play would strengthen autonomy?
3. Commitment (5 min)
